### Question 10 & 11: Circumradius of Triangle
**Question:** "An isosceles triangle of sides 13cm, 13cm, 10cm is inscribed in a circle. What is the radius?"

**Calculation:**
- Triangle sides: a = 13, b = 13, c = 10
- First, find the area using Heron's formula:
  - Semi-perimeter s = (13 + 13 + 10)/2 = 18
  - Area = √[s(s-a)(s-b)(s-c)]
  - Area = √[18 × 5 × 5 × 8]
  - Area = √[3600] = 60 sq.cm

- Circumradius R = (abc)/(4 × Area)
- R = (13 × 13 × 10)/(4 × 60)
- R = 1690/240
- R = 7.041666... cm
- R ≈ 7.04 cm or 7.1 cm

**CURRENT ANSWER: E (6.9cm) - INCORRECT**
**CORRECT ANSWER: A (7.1cm or 7.1124cm)**

**Source:** Circumradius formula R = abc/(4A) from trigonometry

### Question 20: Trigonometric Identity
**Question:** "If sin²(x)=a/b, what is sin(90-x)?"

**Calculation:**
- sin(90° - x) = cos(x)  [Co-function identity]
- Given: sin²(x) = a/b
- Using Pythagorean identity: sin²(x) + cos²(x) = 1
- cos²(x) = 1 - sin²(x) = 1 - a/b = (b-a)/b
- cos(x) = √[(b-a)/b]

**CURRENT ANSWER: C (sqrt((b-a)/b)) - ✓ CORRECT**

**Source:** Trigonometric co-function and Pythagorean identities
